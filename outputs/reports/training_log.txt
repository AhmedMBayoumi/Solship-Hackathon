[EXP-FORECAST] Tabular ensemble (LightGBM, XGBoost) trained on Modal Tesla T4
  Train data : 2024 Jan-Dec excl. Apr+Sep (28,704 rows × 39 features)
  Val data   : 2024 Apr+Sep (5,760 rows)  -- mirror of test months
  Test data  : 2025 Apr+Sep (5,760 rows)
  Features   : 39 (lags 1/4/8/96/192/672, rolling means/std, Fourier 24h/12h/8h/annual,
                   weather: temp/rad/cloud/RH/HDD/CDD, calendar, tariff_enc, buy_price)

  LightGBM (Optuna 80 trials, T4 GPU, 30 min wall):
    Best val NRMSE (Optuna) : 57.82%
    Final val NRMSE         : 43.94%   (train+val for final fit)
    Test NRMSE Apr+Sep 2025 : 64.23%

  XGBoost (Optuna 60 trials, 25 min wall):
    Best val NRMSE (Optuna) : 57.85%
    Final val NRMSE         : 35.97%
    Test NRMSE Apr+Sep 2025 : 66.37%

  LSTM (PyTorch seq2seq, 96-step, T4 GPU, 70s wall):
    Val NRMSE step-1   : 80.07%
    Val NRMSE 96-step  : 85.12%
    Decision: REJECTED — under-performs tabular models due to fewer features.

  Note on test NRMSE: actual mean(load_kw) on Apr+Sep 2025 = 0.901 kW
                      vs annual mean 1.476 kW. RMSE ~0.58 kW gives 64% NRMSE.
  Forecast bias is well-calibrated (mean(pred-actual) = +0.024 for LGBM, +0.026 for XGB).

[OPT-FIX-1] Found and fixed 2 bugs in mpc_loop.py
  Bug A: Grid clamping had wrong sign
    Original: if p_g > 6: p_bat -= delta  (made grid violation WORSE)
    Fixed   : if p_g > 6: p_bat += delta  (correctly increases discharge)
    Same fix applied to p_g < -6 case.
    Impact: ~5 EUR improvement on bills (was reporting clipped p_g without
            adjusting battery, violating energy balance).

  Bug B: LP horizon used forecast for current step
    Per hackathon rules: "At each step t: 1. Observe current SoC, actual load,
    actual PV, actual prices.  2. Forecast load for next H steps."
    Original: load_fc[0] = forecast for current step
    Fixed   : load_fc[0] = ACTUAL load for current step (observable at t)
    Impact: HUGE — moved bill from EUR +8.38 to EUR -19.16 at H=96.

[OPT-MPC] Final controller — rolling-horizon MPC with LP relaxation
  Solver         : scipy.optimize.linprog (HiGHS)  -- replaces CVXPY (Windows crash)
  LP variables   : 5H + 1   (p_chg, p_dis, p_imp, p_exp per step + soc[0..H])
  No binary vars : LP suffices because round-trip eff < 100%
  Wall time      : H=96 in 60s, H=192 in 110s, H=672 in 510s for Apr+Sep
  Energy balance : verified zero error in saved dispatch (max|err|=0.0000)
  Bound checks   : 0 violations of |p_bat|<=8, |p_grid|<=6, soc in [0,1]

[FINAL] Bills on April + September 2025
  Baseline A (existing controller) : EUR  -7.44   (target to beat)
  Baseline B (no battery)          : EUR +68.74
  Our MPC LightGBM H= 96           : EUR -19.16   (savings vs A: +11.72 EUR / +157.7%)
  Our MPC LightGBM H=192           : EUR -21.84   (savings vs A: +14.40 EUR / +193.6%)
  Our MPC LightGBM H=672           : EUR -22.53   (savings vs A: +15.09 EUR / +203%)
  Oracle (perfect foresight) H=96  : EUR -20.14   (forecast-error gap H=96: 0.98 EUR)
  Oracle (perfect foresight) H=192 : EUR -23.45   (gap H=192: 1.61 EUR)
  Oracle (perfect foresight) H=672 : EUR -24.84   (gap H=672: 2.31 EUR)

  Knee analysis: H=192 captures 95% of oracle gain at 1/5 the wall time of H=672.
  Recommended deployment: H=192 (4 days lookahead, ~110 s for 2 months).
